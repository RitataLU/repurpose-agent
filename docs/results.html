05 — Methodology

Spearman correlation metric. Each gene in the 179-gene scored pool receives two rankings: one from the scorer formula (which the agent optimizes) and one from its cosine similarity to the gold standard centroid (the mean normalized feature vector of the 13 curated drug targets). Spearman r measures how closely these two rankings agree — a value of +1 means the scorer ranks genes in exactly the same order as their biological similarity to known drug targets, and 0 means the ranking is random. This metric is meaningful even when no gold gene appears in the top K, because it measures ordering quality over all 179 genes simultaneously.

What the agent does. For each of 10 pre-defined experiments, the agent rewrites scorer.py with a different weight vector and optional interaction terms, evaluates Spearman r, and keeps the change only if it improves. The search space spans causal features (Mendelian randomization z-score, eQTL effect size), druggability signals (Open Targets tractability), and GWAS replication strength. Experiment 5 — adding an eQTL × tissue-specificity interaction term plus a disease burden multiplier — produced the best improvement (+0.0087 above baseline).
